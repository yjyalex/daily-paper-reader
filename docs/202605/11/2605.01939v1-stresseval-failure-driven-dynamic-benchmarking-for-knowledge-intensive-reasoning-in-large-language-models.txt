# Abstract 

Static benchmarks for LLMs are increasingly com-1

promised by contamination and overfitting, es-2

pecially on knowledge-intensive reasoning tasks. 3

While recent dynamic benchmarks can alleviate 4

staleness, they often increase difficulty at the ex-5

pense of answerability and controllability. In 6

this paper, we propose S TRESS EVAL , a failure-7

driven data synthesis framework that turns ob-8

and controlable test instances. S TRESS EVAL con-10 

sists of three stages: (i) it constructs a semi-11

lenging dynamic benchmark. Across several state-22 

of-the-art LLMs, D YNAMIC -O NE EVAL yields sub-23 

stantially larger performance drops than the orig-24 

inal benchmarks while retaining explicit difficulty 25 

factors, enabling more actionable iteration. 26

## 3.3 Multi-criterion Gating 264 

In this stage, we perform multi-criterion gating to control the 265 

quality of synthesized instances. For each candidate instance 266 

xi = ( Si, Qi, Ai, Ψi), we query two LLM-based reviewers, 267 

each prompted to return a structured verdict together with a 268 

calibrated confidence score. Specifically, we use: (1) an An-269 

swerability reviewer, which verifies that the question is an-270 

swerable under the intended stress type and that the gold an-271 

swer is properly grounded—i.e., supported by explicit span-272 

s/records in Si for reasoning-stress, or demonstrably requir-273 

ing a missing external fact beyond Si for knowledge-stress; 274 

and (2) a Consistency reviewer, which attempts to solve the 275 

instance, checks internal consistency among Si, Qi, Ai, and 276 

verifies that the difficulty card Ψi is actually instantiated 277 

rather than merely stated. 278 

Each reviewer outputs a binary decision with confidence. 279

## 4.1 Seed Data & Implementation Details 289 

To initialize D YNAMIC -O NE EVAL , we draw seed instances 290 

from widely used benchmarks. a) Text Reasoning: Hot- 291 

potQA [Yang et al. , 2018] is a Wikipedia-based multi-hop 292 

QA benchmark that requires combining evidence across mul- 293 

tiple documents to answer. b) KG Reasoning: Com- 294 

plexWebQ [Talmor and Berant, 2018] is a compositional 295 

KBQA benchmark designed to answer complex questions by 296 

executing multi-relation queries over a knowledge graph. c) 297 

Table Reasoning: WikiTableQuestion (WTQ) [Pasupat and 298 

Liang, 2015] TabFact is a table-based fact verification dataset 299 

that asks whether a natural-language statement is entailed or 300 

contradicted by a given Wikipedia table. 301 

We generated seed failure cases E by prompting GPT- 302 

5.2 [OpenAI, 2025], a strong LLM , to produce chain-of- 303 

thought [Wei et al. , 2022] answers and then verifying its out- 304 

puts to collect instances where the model erred. For end- 305 

to-end quality control, the strong GPT-5.2 model was used 306 

throughout the StressEval pipeline for fana , fcan , fext , fknow , 307 

fU, fU. All API calls used deterministic decoding with tem- 308 

perature was set to 0.0, top-p was set to 0.95 , and a maximum 309 

token limit was set to 2048 . For each bad case e ∈ E , Stres- 310 

sEval will synthesize 5 ∼ 10 instances. In addition, we do 311 

not synthesize knowledge-stress instances for table reason- 312 

ing, since the case of the WTQ dataset already contain all 313 

evidence required to answer the questions. Detailed prompts 314 

are listed in Appendix A. 315

## 4.4 Human Evaluation 346 

We conducted a lightweight human evaluation to assess the 347 

quality of D YNAMIC -O NE EVAL . We randomly sampled 50 348 

instances spanning both knowledge-stress Γk and reasoning-349 

stress Γr , covering diverse root-cause labels γ ∈ Γ, and 350 

asked three annotators to provide judgments along three di-351 

mensions. First, answerability (A) evaluates whether the in-352 

stance can be resolved from the provided context: for Γr ,353 

whether the gold answer A is supported by explicit context 354 

in S; for Γk, whether solving the question Q necessarily re-355 

quires an external fact missing from S. Second, unambigu-356 

ity (U) assesses whether a unique gold answer exists. Third, 357 

faithfulness to difficulty cards (F) measures whether the diffi-358 

culty card Ψ is correctly instantiated, meaning that the speci-359 

fied bottleneck step and trigger are present in the instance. 360 

The results are reported in the right panel of Table 1. Over-361 

all, human judgments show consistently high A and U across 362 

splits, indicating that instances are generally solvable from 363 

the provided context and admit a unique gold answer. In con-364 

trast, F exhibits larger variance, with reasoning-stress splits 365 

showing notably lower faithfulness (e.g., 88 .0% ∼ 96 .0%) 366 

than knowledge-stress splits (up to 98 .0%), suggesting that 367 

mismatches more often arise from imperfect instantiation of 368 

the intended bottleneck and trigger rather than from missing 369 

fact or ambiguity. Aggregated over the benchmark, the strong 370 

overall scores support the reliability of our framework. 371

## 5.3 Ablation Studies 415 

We fix GPT-5.2 as the synthesis backbone, generate in-416 

stances under each ablated setting, and evaluate the resulting 417 

datasets using DeepSeek V3.2, together with human judg-418 

ments on the corresponding instances. Here, we consider 419 

three ablation groups. Pipeline-level ablations remove ei-420 

ther (a) error analysis , to test whether bottleneck identifi-421 

cation and trigger localization are necessary for controllable 422 

synthesis, or (b) criterion gating , to quantify its role in en-423 

forcing grounding, answerability, unambiguity, and difficulty 424 

preservation. Knowledge-stress ablations remove either (c) 425 

the missing-fact black box ∆K, to test whether explicitly 426 

modeling atomic missing facts stabilizes reliance on exter-427 

nal knowledge, or (d) context freezing , allowing S to be 428 

rewritten and potentially filling the intended knowledge gap. 429 

Reasoning-stress ablations remove either (e) virtual con-430 

text S∗ construction, to assess whether entity virtualization 431 

prevents parametric-memory shortcuts, or (f) the reasoning 432 

skeleton , to test whether explicitly instantiating the bottleneck 433 

and injecting traps improves faithfulness and grounding. 434 

Table 3 reports the ablation results for S TRESS EVAL ,435 

showing that its components must operate in concert to pre-436 

serve both difficulty and validity. At the pipeline level, 437 

removing error analysis substantially degrades performance 438 

(e.g., DeepSeek V3.2 drops from 27 .7% overall in Table 2) 439 

and sharply reduces human-rated faithfulness, indicating that 440 

bottleneck and trigger are crucial for controllable synthesis. 441 

Removing criterion gating also consistently lowers human 442 

faithfulness, confirming its role in enforcing grounding, un-443 

ambiguity, and difficulty preservation. For K-stress, disabling 444 

the missing-fact black box K produces the most severe fail-445 

ures, while unfreezing S weakens the intended knowledge 446 

gap, consistent with context leakage. For R-stress, remov-447 

ing the virtual context S∗ or the two-step reasoning-skeleton 448 

procedure yields instances that are noticeably easier and less 449 

faithful, supporting the claim that virtualization mitigates 450 

parametric-memory shortcuts and that an explicit skeleton en-451 

forces context-grounded reasoning. 452

# 6 Related Work 480 

Knowledge-Intensive Reasoning Benchmarks Early suc-481 

cess of LLMs, exemplified by models like GPT-4 [OpenAI, 482 

2023] and DeepSeek [Liu et al. , 2024], demonstrated impres-483 

sive capabilities across a spectrum of language understanding 484 

and reasoning tasks. However, conventional static evaluation 485 

practices have struggled to keep pace with the rapid evolu-486 

tion of these models. Issues such as benchmark contamina-487 

tion [Deng et al. , 2024; Dong et al. , ], where models may 488 

memorize test data present in their vast training corpora, lead 489 

to an inflated sense of performance and obscure genuine gen-490 

eralization abilities [Lin et al. , 2024b]. This problem is par-491 

ticularly acute in knowledge-intensive settings [Chen et al. ,492 

2025a; Chen et al. , 2022], where correctness relies on up-to-493 

date facts, strict grounding, and robust multi-step inference, 494 

rather than superficial pattern matching [Jiang et al. , 2024]. 495 

The need for benchmarks that can genuinely assess an LLM’s 496 

ability to reason with and apply external knowledge, rather 497 

than merely recall memorized patterns, remains a significant 498 

challenge. Our work directly addresses this by creating in-499 

stances that expose specific knowledge gaps and reasoning 500 

failures that are otherwise masked by existing benchmarks. 501 

Dynamic LLM Benchmarks Prior efforts in automatic 502 

benchmarking [Wang et al. , 2025] broadly fall into four cate-503 

gories: (i) static, human-curated benchmark suites; (ii) LLM-504 

as-judge evaluation frameworks such as MT-Bench [Zheng 505 

et al. , 2023]; (iii) agent-driven, end-to-end automated arenas 506 

(e.g., Auto-Arena [Zhao et al. , 2025]); and (iv) in-the-wild ro- 507 

bustness benchmarks mined from real user interactions, such 508 

as WildBench [Lin et al. , 2024a]. These approaches improve 509 

scalability and realism but have complementary drawbacks: 510 

static benchmarks saturate, risk data contamination, and of- 511 

fer limited diagnostics; mined in-the-wild sets reveal real 512 

failures but lack controllable latent factors for causal stress 513 

tests [Valiulla, 2025]; and fully automated agent pipelines 514 

boost throughput and ranking but may generate cases that 515 

are not reliably answerable, controlable, or causally faith- 516 

ful—limiting debugging and improvement [Shukla, 2025]. 517 

Model-in-the-loop, continual, and adversarial test-generation 518 

frameworks aim to keep evaluations up to date and to un- 519 

cover brittle heuristics that static benchmarks can miss [White 520 

et al. , 2024; Li et al. , 2024]. However, without strict 521 

constraints on answerability and provenance, such dynamic 522 

generation can drift toward unanswerable, underspecified, 523 

or spurious examples, weakening both the validity and the 524 

actionability of the resulting evaluations [Li et al. , 2024; 525 

White et al. , 2024]. Compared with these benchmarks, our 526 

STRESS EVAL prioritizes causal attribution, difficulty preser- 527 

vation, contamination resistance. 528 

# 7 Conclusion 529 

We present S TRESS EVAL , a failure-driven pipeline that con- 530 

verts observed LLM failures into dynamic, answerable, and 531 

high-difficulty evaluation instances through structured er- 532 

ror analysis, dual-perspective instance synthesis, and multi- 533 criterion gating. Instantiated from knowledge-intensive seed 534 

datasets, S TRESS EVAL produces grounded and controlable 535 

tests that induce substantially larger, and more diagnostic, 536 

performance drops across frontier models than standard static 537 

benchmarks, while preserving explicit difficulty factors to 538 

support actionable iteration. Future work will investigate how 539 

different combinations of LLMs within the same S TRESS E-540

You are given: - A question - Context passages with titles and numbered sentences - A model's predicted answer - The model's short explanation and raw output (may be incomplete) Your task: Reconstruct a plausible STRUCTURED reasoning trace that matches the model's predicted answer, using ONLY the provided contexts as evidence. Output MUST be exactly ONE valid JSON object with this schema: {"trace": [{"step": 1, "op": "<one of: bridge_entity, coreference_resolution, constraint_tracking, comparison, attribute_lookup, set_aggregation, distractor_filtering>", "evidence": ["<title>", <sent_id>], "result": "<short intermediate string>" }, ... ]}Rules (CRITICAL): 1) Use ONLY the provided contexts. EVIDENCE-RESULT CONSISTENCY (MOST IMPORTANT): 8) Each step's result MUST be directly supported by its evidence sentence: - Prefer copying a short span that appears verbatim in the evidence sentence. - If minor normalization is needed (e.g., removing parentheses, quotes, or trailing punctuation), keep the surface form essentially identical. - Do NOT invent new entities or facts that do not appear in the cited sentence. 9) Use the op field honestly: - attribute_lookup: evidence states an attribute/value for an entity. - bridge_entity: evidence provides the link entity needed to jump to another title. - coreference_resolution: evidence clarifies what a pronoun/alias refers to. - constraint_tracking: evidence introduces or applies a constraint (year, role, membership, \only", \first", etc.). - comparison: evidence provides a comparable attribute (year/size/rank) used to compare. - set_aggregation: evidence provides membership of a set (list, group, categories). - distractor_filtering: evidence is tempting but not the true support; use it to reflect the model’s likely anchoring. 10) If the predicted answer cannot be supported by any evidence sentence without inventing facts:
