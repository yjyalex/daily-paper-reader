Abstract 

Large language models (LLMs) often suffer from hallucinations due to error accumula-tion in autoregressive decoding, where sub-optimal early token choices misguide subse-quent generation. Although multi-path decod-ing can improve robustness by exploring alter-native trajectories, existing methods lack princi-pled strategies for determining when to branch and how to regulate inter-path interactions. We propose Adaptive Path-Contrastive Decod-ing (APCD), a multi-path decoding frame-work that improves output reliability through adaptive exploration and controlled path in-teraction. APCD consists of two compo-nents: (1) Entropy-Driven Path Expansion, which delays branching until predictive uncer-tainty—measured by Shannon entropy over top candidate tokens—indicates multiple plausible continuations; and (2) Divergence-Aware Path Contrast, which encourages diverse reason-ing trajectories while dynamically attenuating inter-path influence as prediction distributions diverge. Experiments on eight benchmarks demonstrate improved factual accuracy while maintaining decoding efficiency.Our code is available at https://github.com/zty-king/APCD.

Although Diversity Beam Search (DBS)(Vijayakumar et al., 2016) mitigates this, its rigid diversity penalties often disrupt the semantic continuity of reason-ing steps, leading to fragmented inference and de-graded quality. Consequently, developing a strategy that enables semantic-aware multi-path coordina-tion with dynamic, efficient interventions remains a critical open challenge. To bridge these gaps and improve output reli-ability, we propose Adaptive Path-Contrastive Decoding (APCD) , a novel multi-path decoding strategy featuring adaptive path expansion and con-trastive interaction (Figure 1). Our approach builds on two key mechanisms: (1) Entropy-Driven Path Expansion: To avoid ineffective exploration caused by premature branching, APCD initializes decod-ing with a single path and expands into multiple paths only when predictive uncertainty—measured by normalized Shannon entropy over the top-k can-didates—exceeds a predefined threshold, thereby ensuring effective multi-path exploration. (2) Divergence-Aware Path Contrast: To prevent pre-mature path collapse and excessive coupling among paths, APCD introduces contrastive interaction be-tween paths to encourage exploration of distinct reasoning trajectories. As the Jensen–Shannon di-vergence (JSD) between the token prediction distri-butions of contrasted paths increases, the strength of inter-path interaction is dynamically attenuated and eventually halted. After generation completes, the path with the lowest average perplexity is se-lected as the final output, as this metric reflects the logical coherence and global model confidence of the generated trajectory. Figure 2 presents an illus-trative example demonstrating how APCD effec-tively mitigates mode collapse while avoiding the quality degradation caused by continuous, accumu-lated penalties, thereby safeguarding the reliability of the final reasoning outcome. We conduct extensive experiments on multiple large language model architectures (LLaMA-2-7B, LLaMA-2-13B, LLaMA-3-8B, Qwen-II-Medical-8B) and eight benchmark datasets (NEJM, MedM-CQA, MedQA,MedB-4, TruthfulQA, TriviaQA, HotPotQA, Natural Questions). Compared with various decoding strategies (Greedy, Sample, CS, CAD, Beam Search, DBS, ITI, DoLa,AD), APCD consistently improves factual correctness and truth-fulness while maintaining computational efficiency. Our main contributions are as follows: • We introduce an Entropy-Driven Path Expan-sion mechanism that monitors early-step uncer-tainty to identify reliable branching points, en-abling effective and well-timed path expansion in multi-path decoding. • We propose a novel Divergence-Aware Path Contrast mechanism that dynamically adjusts con-trastive strength and detects when to stop contrast, promoting effective exploration while avoiding un-necessary interference. • We conduct extensive experiments across mul-tiple LLMs and datasets, demonstrating that APCD improved factual accuracy while maintaining de-coding efficiency. 

2 Related Works 

2.1 Multi-Path Decoding 

To mitigate the rigidity of greedy generation, multi-path decoding strategies have emerged as a power-ful paradigm. Among representative approaches, Beam Search (Freitag and Al-Onaizan, 2017) main-tains high-probability candidates to balance diver-sity and stability but often suffers from mode col-lapse. Diverse Beam Search (DBS) (Vijayaku-mar et al., 2016) introduces diversity penalties to alleviate this issue; however, its fixed penalty weight struggles to adapt to different reasoning stages. To address the myopia of token-level gen-eration, sentence- or stage-level Beam Search vari-ants (Chen and Liu, 2024; Xu et al., 2024) extend exploration to coarser granularities, though they often remain constrained by the local optimality of the selected branches. Furthermore, sampling-based strategies like Best-of-N (Sun et al., 2024) and Self-Consistency(Wang et al., 2022) generate multiple independent trajectories to select the opti-mal answer via reward models or majority voting. However, these methods incur high computational costs due to redundant sampling and lack mech-anisms to dynamically correct errors during the reasoning process (Xie et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2024a) 

Our Innovation: We introduce an Entropy-Driven Path Expansion mechanism for multi-path decoding. During the early decoding stage, it lever-ages predictive uncertainty to identify informative branching points, ensuring comparable confidence across paths before expansion, and thereby improv-ing the effectiveness of multi-path exploration. 

2.2 Logit-Modulation Decoding 

Distinct from multi-path decoding strategies, logit-modulation approaches focus on refining the prob-ability distribution of token during generation. Specifically, Contrastive Search (CS) (Su and Col-lier, 2022) penalizes repetitive candidates to pre-vent degeneration. Contrastive Decoding (CD) (Li et al., 2023b) amplifies the divergence between an expert and an amateur model to mitigate hallucina-tions, while Context-aware Decoding (CAD) (Shi et al., 2024) contrasts context-aware and context-agnostic logits to enforce prompt adherence. From the perspective of internal states, several methods intervene in or leverage intermediate acti-vations. Inference-Time Intervention(ITI) (Li et al., 2023a) boosts attention heads aligned with truth-fulness directions. Decoding by Contrasting Lay-ers(DoLa) (Chuang et al., 2023) contrasts logits from different layers to isolate factual knowledge, and Activation Decoding (AD) (Chen et al., 2024b) leverages In-Context Sharpness at specific informa-tive layers to improve factuality in text generation. 

Our Innovation: Unlike existing methods that rely on external expert model supervision or man-ual selection of contrastive sublayers, APCD per-forms adaptive cross-path contrast in a multi-path setting. By dynamically adjusting the contrastive strength and determining when to terminate inter-path interaction, it enables fine-grained integration of cross-path information, improving reasoning ro-bustness and accuracy without introducing addi-tional model overhead. For more related work, please refer to Appendix A.1. 

3 Adaptive Path-Contrastive Decoding 

The proposed Adaptive Path-Contrastive Decod-ing (APCD) framework aims to improve the re-liability and interpretability of LLM generation through adaptive multi-path exploration and adap-tive path-level contrastive interaction. As illus-trated in Figure 3, APCD consists of two core com-ponents: Entropy-Driven Path Expansion (EDPE) and Divergence-Aware Path Contrast (DAPC). De-coding is initialized with a single path, and un-certainty over the top candidate tokens is contin-uously monitored by entropy to identify appropri-ate points for path expansion, enabling exploration only when it is warranted. Once decoding tran-sitions from a single-path to a multi-path regime, Divergence-Aware Path Contrast dynamically ad-justs each path’s token prediction distribution by contrasting it with those of other active paths. The strength of inter-path interaction is adaptively mod-ulated based on the Jensen–Shannon divergence (JSD) between prediction distributions, promoting effective exploration while avoiding unnecessary interference. Finally, the output is selected as the path with the lowest perplexity.

5 Conclusion 

We present Adaptive Path-Contrastive Decoding (APCD) to improve the reliability of large language models. APCD combines Entropy-Driven Path Expansion, which delays branching until multiple plausible continuations emerge, with Divergence-Aware Path Contrast, which encourages diverse rea-soning while dynamically regulating inter-path in-fluence. Experiments show APCD enhances factual accuracy and maintains efficient decoding, offering a practical approach for robust LLM deployment. 

Limitations 

APCD enhances the reliability of generative mod-els in high-precision tasks through its two core components: Entropy-Driven Path Expansion and Divergence-Aware Path Contrast. It is important to note that both components rely on sufficient reason-ing length to be fully effective: the Entropy-Driven Path Expansion needs to reach a stable threshold, and Divergence-Aware Path Contrast depends on stepwise updating of contrastive weights during generation. Therefore, APCD still has notable lim-itations in ultra-short text scenarios. Tighter inte-gration of APCD with the model’s hidden states across layers remains a promising direction for fu-ture research. 

Ethical Considerations 

Our work only proposes a decoding strategy and does not introduce new data or modify model train-ing. Therefore, it does not raise additional ethical concerns beyond those inherent to the underlying model. Crucially, while we evaluate our method on medical datasets, these experiments are solely for benchmarking generation quality. The model outputs should not be interpreted as professional medical advice or used for clinical diagnosis.

A.1 More Related Work Analysis 

The most relevant work to this study is Diversity Beam Search (DBS)(Vijayakumar et al., 2016). Ini-tially, the Beam Search algorithm was mainly used for decoding sequence models and applied in tasks like machine translation. With the rapid develop-ment of Large Language Models (LLM), DBS has attracted more attention and has been adapted to Transformer models in machine translation. While Beam Search performs a standard search for the best output, DBS adopts a diverse path strategy to explore multiple paths during decoding, which leads to more diverse and potentially useful solu-tions. This contrast with Beam Search helps to generate outputs that are more diverse and bet-ter aligned with the task’s requirements.However, DBS primarily focuses on diversity, neglecting the coherence of the paths, which may lead to a de-crease in output quality. To address this, we pro-pose the APCD method, which adjusts the contrast wight based on the JSD between the contrasted paths, ensuring that diverse paths maintain both distinctness and coherence. APCD mitigates the is-sues seen in DBS, maintaining high diversity while improving reasoning accuracy, which is key to solv-ing problems encountered with DBS. In recent years, the concept of contrast has gained attention in the development of large-scale model decoding, particularly in the direct use of layer-level logits for achieving practical advance-ments. A typical example is DoLa (Chuang et al., 2023), which enhances factual correctness by con-trasting the projected logits from different hidden layers. Specifically, DoLa exploits the insight that factual knowledge emerges in later layers while ear-lier layers encode syntax. By subtracting the logits of premature layers from the final layer, it effec-tively filters out generic linguistic noise, ultimately offering a new direction for faithful reasoning. Inspired by DoLa, Activation Decoding (Chen et al., 2024b) proposes a method that decodes infor-mation by manipulating the activation matrix , with dimensions input length by word size, applying it to each token’s position in the input. By calculating the Shannon entropy of the logits for each input’s position, it achieves a measure of certainty (with lower entropy values signifying higher certainty). During generation, this method adjusts the logits through logits + = α · (−entropy ), fine-tuning the output to suppress high-entropy words and select those with lower uncertainty. Meanwhile, the re-cently published decoding method ECO Decod-ing(Shin et al., 2025), adjusts the logits of models based on their class probability distributions at each generation step. Furthermore, DeCoRe(Gema et al., 2024) introduces a strategy for comparing base models and finetuned models’ outputs, reducing the likelihood of generating incoherent or mislead-ing results. These methods enhance the decoding process and improve the alignment of output confi-dence with model performance. Building on insights from prior work, we pro-pose the APCD method, which employs two key mechanisms: Entropy-Driven Path Expansion and Divergence-Aware Path Contrast. Entropy-Driven Path Expansion estimates the model’s uncertainty by computing the entropy of the probability dis-tribution over the top-k candidate tokens at each decoding step. A high top-k entropy indicates that the multiple plausible predictions compete, mak-ing exploration particularly valuable, in which case the decoding process branches to explore multiple potential generation paths. Conversely, when the entropy is low, meaning the model’s predictions are concentrated and confident, decoding continues along the main path without branching.By adopting this strategy, APCD can fully exploit its exploration value, leading to improved diversity and better po-tential generation quality.Divergence-Aware Path Contrast enhances generation diversity by contrast-ing each path against others, while dynamically adjusting the contrastive weights to ensure the qual-ity of each path. Our experimental results further demonstrate that APCD effectively improves over-all generation performance, achieving a strong bal-ance between diversity and coherence. This section analyzes the feasibility of the Entropy-Driven Path Expansion through theoret-ical discussion and data analysis. As shown in Figure 5, the model typically exhibits a relatively stable phase in the early stage of reasoning, during which the probability distribution is more concen-trated and the entropy is lower, indicating that the model has higher confidence in the selected to-ken. Subsequently, the model enters an exploration phase, where the probability distribution becomes more uniform and the entropy increases. If path branching is performed during this stable phase, the model is likely to select extremely low-probability tokens too early, leading to the creation of noisy paths. Such spurious trajectories often diverge from plausible reasoning patterns, consuming substan-tial decoding budget without contributing useful alternatives, thereby reducing inference efficiency. More critically, the erroneous tokens introduced during premature branching can be self-reinforcing under standard beam search, leading noisy paths to dominate subsequent selections and ultimately result in incorrect reasoning outcomes. In con-trastive multi-path settings, these spurious paths may further contaminate inter-path interactions by injecting misleading signals into the contrastive updates, which can propagate errors to otherwise reliable paths and degrade the correctness of the fi-nal prediction.To address this, APCD introduces an Entropy-Driven Path Expansion mechanism that de-lays the branching point, preventing the exploration of early noisy paths and enhancing the reliability of multi-path reasoning. As can be seen from the start of outputs for the four questions in Table 6 (corresponding to Fig-ure 5), the model generates several tokens in the ini-tial phase that are completely consistent. This phe-nomenon stems from the model’s training patterns: the model tends to select highly confident and de-terministic tokens in the early stage, resulting in minimal diversity. Consequently, performing path expansion at this stage yields limited exploratory value and may instead introduce noise, thereby de-grading the reliability of the candidate sequences. Thus, branching at the very beginning of decoding is inappropriate. Effective multi-path reasoning should only commence once the entropy of the top-k token distribution is high enough and the probabil-ity distribution becomes more uniform—ensuring that each path maintains both diversity and high generation quality. Our experimental results fur-ther validate the importance and feasibility of the Entropy-Driven Path Expansion in multi-path gen-eration.

C Experiment Implementation details 

Below are some detailed descriptions of the meth-ods used in experiments. The methods include Greedy decoding, Sample (top_p, top_k) decoding, Contrastive Search (CS) decoding, Beam Search decoding, and Diversity Beam Search (DBS) de-coding. We directly invoked the model.generate

num_beam_groups = 3, num_beams = 3, and 

diversity_penalty = 0 .5 to align the beam con-figurations with our multi-path width ( k = 3 ). We use the fine-tuned weights of the Inference-Time Intervention (ITI) models for Llama-2-7b-chat (sourced from honest-llama2-chat-7B), Llama-2-13b-chat (available at honest-llama2-chat-13B), and finally Llama-3-8b-instruct (located at honest-llama3-8B-instruct).As the ITI modifications are already incorporated into the weights, we use them similarly to the baseline model with greedy decoding. For the DoLa method, we use the Hugging Face official implementation via the 

model.generate(...) function during long-text generation experiments and trial with the low and high parameter settings. While for short-text gener-ation, we use the implementation provided by the official code repository (DoLa repository). We fol-lowed the original implementation of the Context Aware Decoding (CAD)(CAD repository) and the Activation Decoding (AD) algorithm (AD reposi-tory). For our APCD, during long-text experiments, we set the number of contrastive paths k = 3 , the initial contrastive weight βmax = 0 .5, the diver-gence threshold δ = 0 .5, and the entropy threshold 

Hθ = 0 .95 . We use the greedy algorithm, which se-lects the token with the highest probability at each step of decoding.At the same time, to accelerate the inference process, we set a perplexity threshold τ = 2 , so that paths exceeding this threshold are no longer continued. We also set α = 0 .2 to fil-ter out low-probability tokens, further speeding up inference. Additional parameters for inference ac-celeration are also used; more details can be found in our code. In short-text experiments, we conduct hyperparameter tuning, and set the parameters for the TruthfulQA dataset and the models Llama-2-7b-chat, Llama-2-13b-chat, and Llama-3-8b-instruct, to the optimal values as: Hθ = 0 .5, k = 15 , H θ =0.6, k = 3, H θ = 0.9, k = 2. For the Triv-ialQA, HotpotQA, and Natural Questions (NQ) datasets, we set that Hθ = 0 .9, k = 2 . Our ex-periments were carried out on four NVIDIA Tesla V100 32GB GPUs. For the TruthfulQA dataset, we keep the model frozen and directly synthesize an-swers, thereby obviating the requirement for back-propagation. The different inference prompts used for various datasets and models are compiled in Appendix F. 

D Evaluation Details 

We evaluate our method on datasets from both gen-eral and medical domains. For the general domain, we use the official validation splits for TriviaQA (11,308 examples), HotPotQA (7,403 examples), and Natural Questions (NQ) (3,603 examples). For TruthfulQA, we utilize the standard test set con-taining 790 examples. For the medical domain, we employ the validation split of MedMCQA (4,183 examples) and the test split of the MedQA-US sub-set (1,273 examples). We also include NEJM (603 examples), sourced from the Medical-Eval-NEJM repository, and MedB-4 (308 examples), derived from the medbullets_op4.json file in the Chal-lengeClinicalQA repository. For the evaluation on the TruthfulQA dataset, we followe the evaluation protocol(Lin et al., 2022). We use the OpenAI API model to evaluate the au-thenticity (Truth), information (Info), and factual information ( T ruth ∩Inf o ) scores, as well as the rejection rate (Reject). Since the OpenAI Curie model is no longer available, we use the OpenAI recommended replacement model, GPT-4o-mini, to train GPT-Judge and GPT-Info models, making sure that the hyperparameters and training data re-main consistent. For the evaluation of TriviaQA, HotpotQA, and Natural Questions (NQ), we adopt the eval-uation method from Joshi (2017), which com-pares the final model output, model_answer, with the ground_truth. If model_answer contains ground_truth, then the answer is considered cor-rect; otherwise, it is considered incorrect. The ac-curacy is then calculated as the EM (Exact Match) score, and the F1 score is computed using the stan-dard formula. The model_answer and ground_truth are split into multiple individual words, and the intersection set U is computed. The precision in-dicates the proportion of U in the model_answer, while recall indicates the proportion of U in the ground_truth. For the NEJM, MedMCQA, and MedQA datasets, we first follow the prompt-guided stan-dard format to retrieve answers. If the model cannot provide the correct answer, we calculate the Strict Match (SM) accuracy, and for those that fail to meet the correct prompt output, the answer output will be considered incorrect. Some errors occur be-cause the II-Medical-8B-1706 model fails to follow the required prompt format during inference, even though the produced answer itself is correct. To address this, we used GPT-4o-mini to post-process the model outputs and extract the intended answers from unformatted responses. If GPT-4o-mini can successfully recover the correct answer, we count it as correct, which constitutes the Fuzzy Match (FM) accuracy. We believe that both the SM and FM accuracy are crucial and valuable. Through SM, not only does it reflect the model’s prompt adherence, but also indicates the model’s ability to adapt to the current prompt method. The FM accu-racy, in contrast, reflects the model’s performance under non-ideal conditions when the model doesn’t strictly follow the prompt.
