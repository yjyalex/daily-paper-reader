## Abstract 

We propose a paradigm shift from learning to answer to learning to question : can a language model generate verifiable problems, solve them, and turn the resulting feedback into self-improvement without human supervision? We introduce AN-CORA , an anchored-curriculum framework in which a unified policy alternates between a Proposer that synthesizes novel specifications and a Solver that pro-duces verified solutions. ANCORA rests on three load-bearing mechanisms: a 

two-level group-relative update that couples Proposer advantages across speci-fications with Solver advantages across solution attempts; iterative self-distilled SFT that projects the base model onto its valid-output manifold before RL; and a 

UCB-guided Curriculum DAG that grows only through strictly filtered, novel, Solver-verified specifications. These stabilizers are necessary because sparse verifier feedback otherwise drives Proposer collapse even under MLRL-aligned rewards. Instantiated in Verus, ANCORA lifts Dafny2Verus pass@1 from a 26.6% SFT baseline to 81.5% in the test-time-training setting under 0-shot evaluation, outperforming the PSV self-play baseline by 15.8 points despite PSV using 1-shot inference; in a separate transfer setting, training from Dafny2Verus seeds yields 36.2% and 17.2% pass@1 on held-out MBPP and HumanEval. 

## 1 Introduction 

Large language models have achieved broad competence via pre-training on web-scale corpora, supervised fine-tuning (SFT), and reinforcement learning from verifiable feedback DeepSeek-AI et al. [2025a]. However, this paradigm faces converging bottlenecks: public human-generated text may become a scaling bottleneck if current data-demand trends continue Villalobos et al. [2024], human annotation does not scale, and imitation-based training caps performance at human level Singh et al. [2024]. Even online RL frameworks that employ external tools Yao et al. [2023] primarily recombine parametric knowledge rather than generating genuinely novel, verifiable discoveries. We propose ANCORA , an anchored-curriculum framework that shifts from learning to answer to 

learning to question . A unified policy πθ alternates between a Proposer that synthesizes novel problem specifications and a Solver that produces verified solutions, coupling both roles through a shared internal model of the problem–solution space. The framework has three load-bearing components: (i) a two-level group-relative update coupling Proposer advantages across candidate specifications with Solver advantages across solution attempts; (ii) iterative self-distilled SFT that lifts the base model onto its valid-output manifold before full RL; and (iii) a UCB-guided Curriculum DAG that grows only through strictly filtered, novel, Solver-verified specifications, allowing the prompt pool to expand without poisoning the valid manifold.

This matters because recent pass@ k analyses suggest that conventional RLVR can mainly sharpen small-k sampling efficiency without expanding, and sometimes while narrowing, the large-k reasoning boundary already present in the base model Yue et al. [2025]. For the Proposer, the goal is complementary: generate specifications near the Solver’s epistemic frontier, where outcome entropy is high enough to create useful Solver learning signal. A central failure diagnosis is manifold collapse : when valid outputs are sparse relative to the full generation space, sparse verifier feedback can push the Proposer off the valid manifold regardless of whether the entropy reward is Bernoulli variance 4p(1 −p), an exponential surrogate e−Kp −e−K , or a Band-1-of-K indicator 1[m=1] (Appendix B). Iterative SFT and the strictly gated UCB-DAG are therefore not auxiliary engineering details; they are the stabilizers that let entropy-targeted Proposer rewards operate without curriculum poisoning. Under these stabilizers, the Band-1-of-K reward is our main-result default; we ablate the other two shapes in Appendix D.3. We validate ANCORA on Verus formal verification Lattuada et al. [2023], where a deterministic compiler provides scalable ground-truth rewards. Compared to prior self-play and self-improvement methods Zhao et al. [2025], Wilf et al. [2025], Chen et al. [2025], ANCORA is distinguished by its combination of coupled two-level optimization, manifold-first initialization, and strictly gated UCB-DAG exploration. Our contributions are: (1) We introduce ANCORA, the first framework, to our knowledge, that integrates a unified-policy Proposer/Solver with the three mechanisms above for verifiable reasoning. (2) We diagnose the manifold collapse problem for sparse-verifier RL and show why manifold projection plus gated anchored exploration is necessary for stable Proposer learning. (3) We empiri-cally demonstrate that a Qwen2.5-Coder-3B model trained with ANCORA significantly outperforms baselines on three Verus benchmarks. 

## 2 Related Work 

Reasoning with Static Prompts. STaR Zelikman et al. [2022], OpenAI o1 OpenAI et al. [2024], and DeepSeek-R1 DeepSeek-AI et al. [2025a] train reasoning via RL on a fixed set of human-curated prompts. While effective at distilling search compute into the policy, they do not expand the problem space itself, and performance is bounded by the cardinality of the initial prompt set. 2Concurrent Self-Play Methods. AZR Zhao et al. [2025] uses a single model for code-based reasoning with a proposer reward of 1 − ¯rsolve and Task-Relative REINFORCE++ Hu [2025]. SPELL Yang et al. [2025b] adds three roles (questioner, responder, verifier) for document QA via summed GRPO losses. SPICE Liu et al. [2025a] applies dual-role Dr. GRPO Liu et al. [2025b] to corpus-grounded math reasoning. SSR Wei et al. [2025] trains a shared bug-injector/solver policy via PPO Schulman et al. [2017] for software engineering. Notably, negative-gradient destabilization is not unique to our setting: Kimina-Prover Wang et al. [2025], training a 72B Lean-4 prover via GRPO, reports early “format collapse” and resorts to probabilistically discarding negative-advantage samples (ω = 0 .5). The underlying phenomenon—thin valid manifolds concentrating repulsion on a few off-manifold modes rather than spreading it across the vast invalid space—is shared by formal proofs, verified specifications, and strictly typed code (Appendix B). Unlike ad-hoc sample discarding or linear inverted rewards, ANCORA prevents format collapse systematically via manifold-constraining SFT and a UCB-DAG-anchored frontier objective. 

Decoupled or Alternating Self-Play. An alternative family maintains separate proposer/solver models or updates them in alternating phases: Self-Questioning LMs Chen et al. [2025] (asymmetric self-play), Dr.Zero Yue et al. [2026] (alternating HRPO/GRPO on separate copies), SOAR Sundaram et al. [2026] (bilevel teacher-student meta-RL), Tool-R0 Acikgoz et al. [2026] (which finds shared weights hurt their tool-calling domain—a finding our coupled spec/implementation setting reverses), STP Dong and Ma [2025] (alternating expert iteration for Lean), and Sol-Ver Lin et al. [2025] (sequential SFT +DPO without RL proposing). These decoupled strategies sidestep gradient conflicts but forfeit the mutual regularization of shared parameters Silver et al. [2017]. 

Formal Verification and Self-Improvement. PSV Wilf et al. [2025] uses an alternating propose-solve loop for Verus. However, its proposer relies on in-context learning rather than RL, and its solver is updated via rejection fine-tuning. DeepSeek-Prover Ren et al. [2025] uses Lean as a static reward model without generating new theorems. In contrast, ANCORA formulates specification generation and solution as a unified RL policy, trained jointly via MLRL-aligned GRPO, repurposing the compiler as a dynamic engine for autonomous problem discovery. 

Positioning. ANCORA’s distinguishing feature is the joint use of all three mechanisms above; Table 3 in the appendix gives a per-feature comparison.

## 4 Methodology 

4.1 The ANCORA Optimization Framework 

ANCORA is a discrete-time dynamic system where a single unified policy πθ serves as both Proposer and Solver. We refer to Proposer rollouts as question/specification generation (Q-Gen) and Solver roll-outs as solution generation (S-Gen); correspondingly, subscripts Q and S denote the Proposer/Q-Gen and Solver/S-Gen branches. At each iteration, the Proposer samples a novel task x′ ∼ πθ (x′|xseed )

from a dynamically expanding Seed Pool S, and the Solver attempts a solution y ∼ πθ (y|x′) judged by a deterministic verifier V(x′, y ) ∈ { 0, 1}. The framework couples the two roles through a shared policy update while admitting new curriculum nodes only after strict filtering, novelty checks, and Solver verification, so that self-expansion remains anchored to the valid manifold. 

4.2 Proposer Policy Strategy 4.2.1 Curriculum Generation 

At each step, a seed xseed is selected from the DAG Seed Pool via UCB sampling (§4.4), and the Proposer generates a one-step curriculum extension. The iterative SFT warm-up teaches the model to make focused semantic changes rather than superficial rewrites, keeping exploration local enough to remain on the valid manifold while still walking toward new frontier regions; prompt details are deferred to Appendix E. 

4.2.2 Filter Gate and Zero-Distortion Rewards 

Generated candidates pass through a lightweight three-stage accept/reject gate (format checks, compiler stub syntax validation, and semantic heuristics rejecting vacuous postconditions); rejected candidates are excluded from the GRPO group. We strictly decouple search from learning : heuristic scores and MinHash novelty Broder [1997] drive only gating and deduplication, while the policy gradient is driven exclusively by binary verifier outcomes cj,k ∈ { 0, 1}. Injecting tiered heuristics into the reward instead induces spurious advantage rankings among invalid outputs and displaces correct-output likelihood mass Deng et al. [2025] (Appendix B). 

4.2.3 Proposer Objective: Entropy-Targeting Reward 

The Proposer must generate specifications whose Solver outcomes are informative rather than degenerate: not saturated ( pj ≈ 1), not purely off-manifold noise, but likely to expose useful MLRL signal for the Solver. Our contribution here is an entropy-targeting reward family : bounded functions of the Solver’s K binary outcomes that turn the empirical outcome distribution itself into the Proposer’s learning signal. 4.4 UCB-Guided Seed Pool and Curriculum DAG 

A single Proposer rollout usually produces only a local variation of its conditioning seed. To reach distant regions without leaving the valid manifold, ANCORA composes such local moves: verified novel specs are reinserted as future seeds, forming a curriculum DAG (Figure 3). Initial curated seeds are protected roots; non-original nodes are admitted only if at least one Solver rollout verifies them ( solved-only admission , Lemma G.1); near-duplicates are rejected by MinHash. Seed selection uses MCTS-style UCB descent Auer et al. [2002], Kocsis and Szepesvári [2006] with a tunable root quota ρ ∈ [0 , 1] : a fraction ρ of the batch is sampled from UCB-chosen roots, and the remaining 

1 − ρ performs UCB descent into Q-Gen-discovered children. Our main run uses ρ = 0 .50 ; the limiting case ρ=1 is reported as the No-Descent ablation (§5.3). Discounted backups, depth-capped absorb-and-merge, and production deviations are formalized in Appendix G.3–G.12.

ANCORA significantly outperforms baselines on Dafny2Verus and MBPP, and remains competi-tive on HumanEval. At step 2,000 (with n=100 rollouts), ANCORA achieves 81.5% pass@1 on Dafny2Verus, a +15 .8-point gain over PSV (1-shot). On MBPP, ANCORA reaches 44.1% pass@1, exceeding PSV by 7.3 points. On HumanEval, ANCORA matches PSV at pass@1 (19.2% vs. 19.1%) and remains within 1.3 points on pass@10 (23.9% vs. 25.2%), reflecting the protocol-controlled tradeoff: ANCORA recovers PSV’s 1-shot pass@1 without any in-context exemplar at inference time. The flat MBPP pass@ k curve (44.1 →47.9 across k=1 →10 ) indicates the policy has sharpened to near-deterministic solutions for solvable problems. On Transfer MBPP, ANCORA’s pass@10 (39.9) is within 1.6 points of PSV (41.5), consistent with the same sharpening pattern: ANCORA concentrates mass at pass@1 (44.1 vs. 25.3 for PSV) at the cost of a slightly narrower sampling tail. The narrower HumanEval margin reflects task-origin domain shift orthogonal to this sharpening: although all three benchmarks are evaluated as Verus code, HumanEval-Verified’s 85 functions are Verus translations of general Python programming tasks (string parsing, list manipulation, arithmetic loops) rather than algorithm-verification specifications native to D2V, so the D2V-seeded Proposer distribution transfers less directly to HumanEval than to MBPP’s algorithmic-task style. This is the price of training the Proposer on a single seed family—transferable problem-solving structure is preserved (HE pass@1 rises from 6.2% SFT init to 17.2% Transfer / 19.2% TTT), but the gain narrows as the evaluation domain drifts further from the seed-induced distribution.

## 6 Discussion and Conclusion 

We have presented ANCORA, a framework for autonomous self-improvement in verifiable reasoning that unifies problem proposing and solving within a single RL policy. The empirical results—81.5% pass@1 on Dafny2Verus at 0-shot, and 36.2% / 17.2% pass@1 on held-out MBPP / HumanEval in a separate Dafny2Verus-seeded transfer run—show that a 3B model can self-bootstrap a verifiable problem distribution from a curated seed set (no human-written solutions in the training corpus) and learn transferable problem-solving structure rather than only fitting the D2V evaluation distribution. The 1-shot Q-Gen / 0-shot S-Gen schedule used here is one instance of a broader dynamic-ICL annealing family analyzed in Appendix C via homotopy continuation. 

Generalizable takeaways for thin-manifold RL. We hypothesize that two ingredients are jointly necessary on any RL task whose valid manifold is exponentially small in sequence length: (i) a manifold-projection phase (here, iterative self-distilled SFT) that lifts pM above 1/N before policy gradients apply, without which Theorems B.1–B.3 and Propositions B.4–B.5 together predict col-lapse; and (ii) a state-conditioned exploration anchor (here, UCB-DAG descent from verified seeds) preventing drift back below this threshold. Concurrent Lean work Wang et al. [2025] reaches a related conclusion by gradient-filtering negative-advantage samples—an instance of the same constraint expressed at the gradient layer rather than the manifold-anchoring layer. 

What ANCORA does not yet address. Our framework prevents manifold-level collapse but does not directly target within-manifold mode collapse : the Proposer can converge to a narrow productive sub-region of the valid manifold even when qvalid stays high (Appendix D.5). Preliminary follow-up 9runs suggest that an explicit entropy auxiliary on the Proposer head, or a verifier-conditioned multi-turn repair loop on the Solver head, are two complementary axes worth investigating; we leave a controlled study to subsequent work. 

Limitations and future work. Our study is compute-limited to a 3B model and a single Verus instantiation; testing the Proposer/Solver paradigm in Lean4 or other verifier-backed domains remains necessary. The main open issue is long-horizon test-time training: an unbounded solved-only Seed Pool should ideally support steady gains, yet our 2,000-step runs plateau after the main phase. The dynamic-ICL annealing analysis in Appendix C is also unvalidated; future runs should test 2–4-shot Q-Gen schedules annealed to 0-shot.

Combined pathology. The net effect is a policy that slowly drifts away from the SFT-initialized manifold along directions determined by a handful of over-penalized negatives.

B.7 Implications for ANCORA 

Theorems B.1–B.5 establish three mechanisms by which RL training collapses when pM ≪ 1/N .The ANCORA framework addresses each: 1. SFT Warm-Up (Breaking Theorem B.1): Fine-tuning on expert demonstrations raises 

pM from near-zero to a level where N p M ≫ 1, ensuring that most groups contain at least one valid sample for positive-advantage gradients. 2. Pure Binary Rewards (Mitigating Theorem B.3): By using binary rewards R ∈ { 0, 1}

with filtering as accept/reject gating (rather than tiered heuristic scores), zero-hit groups produce exactly zero gradient rather than arbitrary off-manifold gradients. This eliminates the most damaging source of directional misalignment. Heuristic scores were found experi-mentally to harm training—a finding consistent with the LLD analysis of Deng et al. [2025], who show that reward noise amplifies likelihood displacement. 3. UCB-DAG Anchoring (Breaking Theorem B.5): By conditioning generation on verified seed specifications, the anchor mechanism constrains exploration to a neighborhood of M

where valid outputs have higher density ( pM ≫ 1/N locally), dramatically reducing the fraction of zero-gradient groups and shortening momentum drift episodes. The UCB selec-tion further keeps training near the Solver’s capability frontier, while the entropy-targeting reward family prevents already-mastered all-pass groups from dominating the Proposer update and, depending on the variant, biases the signal toward symmetric uncertainty or hard nonzero frontier cases. 

## C Homotopy-Continuation Interpretation 

This appendix offers a homotopy-continuation reading of the dynamic-curriculum schedule. D.4 ZPD Upper-Margin Clipping (Negative Result) 

Beyond the default ZPD window [ˆ a, ˆa+δ], we ablated a stricter variant that shrinks the window’s upper end by a fixed margin u, i.e. hi = min(ˆ a+δ−u, 1) , hypothesizing that denying the UCB 20 selector access to near-ceiling seeds would reduce reward-dead proposals and slow Solver entropy collapse. At u=0 .10 (with δ=0 .40 ), at matched step 200 the intervention produces only within-noise suppression of proposer pass rate ( ∆qpass =−0.021 ) and structural similarity ( ∆qstruct =−0.015 ), while imposing a 4–7× cold-start seed-supply deficit: during steps 50 –100 the baseline accrues +516 

new pool entries (a jackpot admission phase) whereas the clipped variant accrues only +75 , and both runs converge to ∼0.7 new seeds/step thereafter. The knob is also structurally time-limited: once ˆa > 1−δ+u (here =0 .70 , crossed near step 150 ), the clamp min( ·, 1) becomes the binding constraint and the margin has no effect. We report this as evidence that late-stage Proposer drift is not remediable by narrowing the ZPD window at admission time; the drift originates downstream in the reward-variance dynamics rather than in the sampling support.

## G Curriculum Bandit Analysis: Discounted UCB on a Lipschitz DAG 

This appendix formalizes the curriculum explorer used by ANCORA as a non-stationary discounted-UCB problem on a Lipschitz arm space , and derives a surrogate regret object that the implementation qualitatively targets. A flat epochized UCB1 framing over an active set Ve would make the vanilla bound vacuous because the online system grows its arm set at every step ( Ke linear in T ). The genuine mathematical object exercised by the production implementation is a discounted UCB recursion on arms that live in an ϵ-packing of a similarity metric space, with solved-only admission guaranteeing boundedness of rewards and with topological hard-keep restricted to being an optional long-horizon cleanup policy rather than a load-bearing theoretical primitive.
